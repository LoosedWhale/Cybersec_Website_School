Cyber Basics



Malware: Harmful software like viruses and ransomware.

Firewall: Security for controlling network traffic.

VPN (Virtual Private Network): Encrypted internet connection for privacy.

Phishing: Deceptive attempts to extract sensitive information.

2FA (Two-Factor Authentication): Dual verification for added security.

Patch/Update: Software modifications for improved security.

Encryption: Converting information for confidentiality.

Antivirus Software: Detects and removes malicious software.


Cyber Threats



Malware disrupts system.

Social engineering manipulates users.

Ransomware encrypts data for extortion.

ZeroDay exploits target unknown vulnerabilities.

Password attacks seek unauthorized access.

DDoS overwhelms systems with traffic.

Phishing pretends be a trusted actor.

Security Tips



Create complex passwords using a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using easily guessable information.

Multi-Factor Authentication aslo know as MFA should be enableld whenever possible for an extra layer of security.

Educate yourself about common online scams and phishing techniques to recognize and avoid them. By doing this you will have a greater understanding of cyber security.

Set a strong password for your Wi-Fi network and use WPA3 also known as Wi-Fi Protected Access 3 as it provides encryption for added protection.

Keep your devices, apps, and antivirus software up to date to fix vulnerabilities. There is a reason why companies realise updates, it's not just to annoy you it also has it's benifits.

Regularly back up important data to an external drive or secure cloud service. If your data is very important to you it should be saved in atleast 3 different places.









Social Media Awareness: Be cautious about the information you share on social media to minimize the risk of social engineering attacks.

Secure Your Devices: Use passcodes or biometric authentication on your mobile devices and install reputable security apps.

Email Vigilance: Verify the legitimacy of emails, avoid clicking on suspicious links, and refrain from sharing personal information unless certain of the source.
