Tips Explanation
Strong Passwords: Create complex passwords using a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using easily guessable information.
Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Enable MFA whenever possible for an extra layer of security.
Stay Informed: Educate yourself about common online scams and phishing techniques to recognize and avoid them.
Secure Wi-Fi: Set a strong password for your Wi-Fi network and use WPA3 encryption for added protection.
Regular Updates: Keep your devices, apps, and antivirus software up to date to fix vulnerabilities.
Backup Your Data: Regularly back up important data to an external drive or secure cloud service.
Social Media Awareness: Be cautious about the information you share on social media to minimize the risk of social engineering attacks.
Secure Your Devices: Use passcodes or biometric authentication on your mobile devices and install reputable security apps.
Email Vigilance: Verify the legitimacy of emails, avoid clicking on suspicious links, and refrain from sharing personal information unless certain of the source.
